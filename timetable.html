Time Table
The story behind a task schedular App to help you manage a perfect routine
To all the ones searching for that first job, this story behind time table might motivate you to take more actions and blah blah blah
After heading back to home as a junior iOS Developer from DevMountain, my whole routine and schedule went off track. I was once discussing this with my friend, who back then was preparing for his military defence exams, and he also stated that he is going through disturbed routine and that has effected his efficiency too. Specifically these are the times when the idea of designing a new app clicks in. I wanted an app to notify me when I have to perform a particular in a particular time and track tasks, and timetable does exactly that!
The main objective of making this app was obviously to help people follow their routine that leads them to reaching their goals faster. Also I was in a job searching phase so I thought a good time schedular app will be a good add on in my portfolio.
Though the App was pretty simple, it was featured as the top 30th app in South African App Store, and it has near 15,000 Downloads.
With so much unexpected growth I've decided to upgrade Time Table for iOS 13, which is coming very soon.
Meanwhile, if you or anyone you know is struggling to keep up with their daily routine, you might check out TimeTable which is available on the App Store for free.

Tech Stack
All the technologies used behind Time Table
  • Swift 4 - Programming Language used for writing Time Table.
  • Firebase Authentication - For user authentication.
  • Firebase Database - For backing up users routine data.
  • RNCryptor - For encrypting & decrypting
    routine data before backup and restore respectively.
  • UNUserNotication (UIKit) - For local notifications.
  • NSUserDefaults - For saving routine data locally.
  • Photoshop - For making app icon & design prototypes.
